Introduction: Everything You Need to Know About Italy.

The southern-central European country,
Italy is known for its beautiful coastline.

Its capital, Rome, attracts many tourists because of its ancient ruins,
amongst many other things.

Italys iconic traditional foods consist of pasta, pizza,and gelato.

Florence is another major city in Italy
known for the Reinissance time period.

Other touristy cities are, Milan,
known as Italys fashion capitol,
and Venice,the romantic city of Canals.

Italy is at the forefront of European affairs and unification.

They joined the Economic and Monetary Union in 1999.

Italy also is a charter member of NATO
and the European Economic Community (EEC)
and its subsequent successors
the EC and the EU.

In 1861 Italy became a nation-state after
King Victor EMMANUEL II united Sardinia,
Sicily, and the regional states of the peninsula.

In the early 1920s Benito MUSSOLINI established a
Fascist dictatorshipin 1946,
ending an era of parliamentary government.

This led to an alliance with Nazi Germany which ultamitately
brang Italy to defeat in World War II.

Post World War II, they became a democratic
republic and economic revival followed.
