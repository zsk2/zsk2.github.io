Introduction: Everything You Need to Know About Italy.

The southern-central European country, Italy is known for its beautiful coastline. Its capital, Rome, attracts many tourists because of its ancient ruins, amongst many other things. Italys iconic traditional foods consist of pasta, pizza,and gelato. Florence is another major city in Italy known for the Reinissance time period that where many signifigant artists pieces still remain. Other touristy cities are, Milan, known as Italys fashion capitol, and Venice, the romantic city of Canals, which also happens to be known by the fact that it is sinking!

Italy is at the forefront of European affairs and unification. They joined the Economic and Monetary Union in 1999. Italy also is a charter member of NATO and the European Economic Community (EEC) and its subsequent successors the EC and the EU.

In 1861 Italy became a nation-state after King Victor EMMANUEL II united Sardinia, Sicily, and the regional states of the peninsula. In the early 1920s Benito MUSSOLINI established a Fascist dictatorshipin 1946, ending an era of parliamentary government. This led to an alliance with Nazi Germany which ultamitately brang Italy to defeat in World War II. Post World War II, they became a democratic republic and economic revival followed.

Economy

Overview

The Italian economy is mainly driven by the manufacture of high-quality consumer goods that are produced by the small/medium-sized enterprises, many of them are family-owned. Italys economly is the third-largest economy in the euro-zone. The underground economy: agriculture, construction, and service sectors, account for 17% of the GDP. There has been many years of tension between the North and the South due to unbalanced economic prosper. The North has developed industries, dominated by private companies. The agricultural South, is less-developed, highly subsidized,and has a long reputation of unemployment and underdevelopment. Its high public debt has continued to increase since 2007, and reached 131% of GDP in 2017.
